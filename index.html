Bio

I am a Ph.D. student in the Robotic Sensor Networks Laboratory, advised by Prof. Volkan Isler. My current research interest lies in visual prospection - the way of learning how to simulate future states of our environment visually. My goal is to allow robotic agents to reason about our world, given visual observations, and simulate possible future scenarios based on the agent's actions. The ability to "pre-experience" events in memory is simple for humans; most people would agree that chocolate desserts taste better with cinnamon than parmesan without having to try both options, but this mental simulation of events is currently impossible for AI. I have previously worked on domain adaptation, point cloud classification, and various problems in precision agriculture.
